Trip overview
🏛️ Athens
Ancient wonders · No car needed · Gateway to the islands
Daily budget
€110–140 / couple
3 nights
🤰 Pregnancy note for Athens: Breakfast is included at Attalos Hotel 📍 all 3 mornings — start every day with a proper meal before going anywhere. Avoid outdoor sightseeing between noon and 3pm — Athens hits 34–38°C in late August. The Acropolis visit is planned for late afternoon (5pm) when it's cooler, less crowded, and the light is beautiful. Carry 1.5L+ water everywhere and go at your own pace.
Getting in
Metro Line 3 from airport direct to Monastiraki — €10/person, 40 min. Skip taxis entirely.
Check in at Attalos Hotel 📍, Monastiraki — confirmed ✅. Drop bags, freshen up. If room isn't ready, front desk will store luggage. Breakfast is included all 3 mornings — served in the hotel daily.
Afternoon
Too hot for the Acropolis today — save it for tomorrow at 8am sharp. Walk to Plaka, wander, get oriented, soak in the city.
Walk through Anafiotika — the tiny island-style neighborhood tucked into the Acropolis hill. You'll feel like you've stumbled into a film set.
Evening
Dinner at Taverna tou Psyrri in the Psyrri neighborhood — one street back from the tourist drag. Mezze plates €5–8, carafe of house wine €8.
Reasonably early night — you've been travelling all day. Tomorrow is a relaxed day but you want to be fresh for the Acropolis at 5pm.
💡 Budget tip
Gyros from a street stall = €2.50. Best meal in Athens. Get one on the walk from the metro — it'll set the tone for the whole trip.
Morning — no rush
Breakfast at Attalos Hotel 📍 (included ✅) — Greek buffet, yogurt and honey, hot options. Take your time, no early alarm today.
Gentle morning wander — Monastiraki Square, Ermou Street shopping, Plaka alleyways. Grab a coffee at one of the many rooftop cafes with Acropolis views.
Browse Monastiraki flea market — leather goods, jewelry, vintage finds. No pressure to buy, great for atmosphere.
Midday — rest
Lunch somewhere cool and shaded — try Tzitzikas kai Mermigas near Syntagma for excellent modern Greek food at fair prices. Two people ~€35–45.
🤰 Back to Attalos by 1pm for a proper rest during peak heat (1–4pm). Non-negotiable in August pregnancy — this is when Athens hits 36–38°C.
Late afternoon — Acropolis at 5pm
Leave hotel at 4:30pm. Walk 10 min to the Acropolis main entrance. Pre-book tickets online (€30/person) — book today if you haven't already.
5pm is genuinely the best time in August — heat has broken, afternoon light is golden on the marble, tour groups have thinned significantly. The Parthenon at this hour is extraordinary.
🤰 Still bring 1L+ water and wear a hat — but the 5pm temperature is 6–8°C cooler than the 8am marble-scorching conditions. Much more manageable for pregnancy.
Take your time — the Acropolis is open until 8pm in summer. Walk out to the Belvedere terrace for the panoramic Athens view and photograph the Parthenon in golden light.
Evening
Walk down through Anafiotika neighborhood — the tiny island-style maze of alleys tucked into the Acropolis hill. Magical in the early evening.
Sunset drinks at Couleur Locale rooftop bar in Monastiraki — Acropolis views, worth the €10 cocktail.
Dinner in Plaka — Strofi restaurant has Acropolis views and great food. Or Diporto near Omonia — no menu, cash only, incredibly delicious. Two people €25–30.
💡 Tip
Pre-book Acropolis tickets online at odysseus.culture.gr — €30/person. The ticket also covers the Ancient Agora and 6 other sites and is valid for 5 days if you want to revisit anything. Skip the Agora on this trip — the Acropolis is the priority and you don't need to rush.
Morning — breakfast then Cape Sounion
Breakfast at Attalos Hotel 📍 (included ✅) — last Attalos breakfast, enjoy it. Then head to Cape Sounion.
KTEL bus from Pedion tou Areos terminal to Cape Sounion — ~90 min, €7/person each way. Aim to depart by 9–9:30am — earlier is cooler but don't stress if it's 10am.
Temple of Poseidon: clifftop ruin 60m above the Aegean — one of the most dramatic ancient sites in Greece. €10 entry. Far less crowded than the Acropolis.
🤰 Less demanding than the Acropolis — partially shaded, open space, plenty of sitting spots. Wear a hat, bring water, walk at your own pace.
Afternoon
Optional swim at one of the small beaches near Sounion on the return bus — Legrena or Varkiza have calm, clean water.
Back in Athens by 3–4pm. Rest at the hotel during the hottest window.
Evening
Browse Monastiraki flea market in the evening cool — leather goods, jewelry, vintage items. Great for a wander.
Dinner in Psyrri — To Kafeneio or Oinoscent for excellent mezze. Two people €30–45.
💡 Tip
The KTEL bus to Sounion is far cheaper than any organised tour. The same bus stops at several beaches on the coast road on the return — a free bonus.
Morning — last Attalos breakfast & checkout
Last Attalos breakfast (included ✅) — eat well, it's a long ferry day. Greek buffet, fuel up properly.
Check out and store bags at the hotel — Attalos will hold luggage until you're ready to leave for Piraeus 📍. Enjoy a last coffee in Monastiraki Square.
Ferry to Naxos
Metro Line 1 from Monastiraki to Piraeus 📍 Port — 35 min, €1.50/person. Your ferry departs 3:00 PM from Piraeus 📍. Plan to arrive at the port by 2:15 PM (45 minutes early).
Blue Star Ferries conventional ferry — BOOKED ✅. Departure: 3:00 PM from Piraeus 📍. Arrival: 7:25 PM in Naxos. Indoor reserved seating recommended for comfort.
🤰 Avoid SeaJets fast ferries on this crossing — smaller, bumpier, harder on pregnancy. Blue Star is much more stable with reclining seats, onboard café, and proper bathrooms.
Arrive Naxos Town 📍 (Chora) evening. Port is right in the center — short taxi to Proteas Hotel & Suites 📍 ✅ near Agios Prokopios. Note: breakfast is not included at Proteas — plan for the hotel breakfast add-on (~€15–20/person) or a local bakery each morning.
💡 Pregnancy tip
🤰 Reserved indoor seating costs €3–5 extra per person but is essential for a comfortable 5-hour crossing when pregnant. Board early to secure a window seat with good ventilation.

🌾 Naxos
Best beaches · Paros day trip (Sep 1) · Most authentic food · Cheapest island
Daily budget
€130–160 / couple
6 nights
🤰 Pregnancy note for Naxos: Naxos beaches are FREE — no forced sunbed fees. Beach by 8:30–9am, mandatory midday break 12–3pm, swim in the gentler morning waves. Carry 2L water daily. Mountain village day is cooler at 400–600m elevation — ideal for August.
Today
Explore Naxos Town 📍 (Chora) — the Venetian Kastro, narrow medieval alleyways, tiny Archaeological Museum (€4/person).
Walk out to the Portara 📍 at sunset — a giant marble doorway from a 530 BC unfinished temple on a small causeway islet. Free, and one of the most romantic spots in the Cyclades.
Waterfront promenade for the evening — compare menus, pick what looks freshest. This is Naxos, the food island of the Cyclades.
Dinner
Maro's Taverna or To Spitiko — authentic home cooking, mains €10–15. Order grilled octopus, horiatiki salad, and tzatziki to share.
💡 Tip
House wine (not bottled) at Greek tavernas is €8–12 a carafe — often better than the bottled options. Always ask for it. Naxos also produces outstanding Graviera cheese — order it wherever you see it.
Today
Agios Prokopios — consistently ranked one of the best beaches in Greece. Turquoise water, white-gold sand, gentle waves perfect for swimming.
🤰 Be on the beach by 8:30–9am. Swimming is excellent at 13 weeks — gentle and cooling. Take a long break in shade between 12–3pm. Consider sunbeds (€20–25/pair) for the guaranteed shade umbrella.
The beach connects south to Agia Anna — a lovely flat coastal walk in the cool late afternoon.
Food
Lunch at a beachfront taverna — fresh grilled fish by the kilo (~€12–18/kg). Confirm feta is pasteurized (almost always is in restaurants). Carry 2L+ water.
💡 Pregnancy tip
🤰 Beach essentials: wide-brim hat, SPF 50+ sunscreen (reapply every 90 min), 2L+ water, light cover-up for shade breaks, healthy snacks. Get out of the sun well before you feel hot — overheating is the main risk.
Today — paced for comfort
Day trip into Naxos's cooler mountainous interior — villages sit at 400–600m elevation, noticeably cooler than the coast. The best choice for a hot August day.
Hire a taxi for the day (€60–70 total) — depart by 9am. Halki first: the Vallindras citron distillery is free to visit and they'll pour you a kitro liqueur tasting.
Apiranthos: marble-paved streets, tiny village museums (€2 each), unchanged village culture. Flat enough to wander at a gentle pace.
🤰 Skip the Filoti–Mt. Zeus hike entirely. Good footwear essential on marble streets. Taxi between villages — don't walk between them in the heat.
Lunch
Eat in Apiranthos at a village kafeneio — slow-cooked lamb or goat, local Graviera cheese, fresh bread. Two people €20–30. One of the best meals of the trip.
💡 Pregnancy tip
🤰 Hire the taxi rather than taking the bus — you can head back whenever you need to without waiting for a scheduled departure. The €60–70 is well spent for comfort and flexibility.
Today
Plaka Beach: long, wild, unspoiled — 4km of free sand, wider and quieter than Agios Prokopios. One of the most beautiful beaches in Greece.
This is your do-nothing day. Swim, read, rest. Greece rewards the unhurried.
🤰 The south end has sunbeds and shade umbrellas (~€15/pair) — worth it today for guaranteed shade. The walk between ends of Plaka is 2km each way — cool morning only.
Evening
Back in Naxos Town 📍. Metaxy Mas or Dal Professore for dinner — Naxian cooking at its best, two people €35–45. Fresh-squeezed juice instead of wine — Greek restaurants always have it and it's excellent.
💡 Pregnancy tip
🤰 This slow day matters more than it looks. Travel fatigue accumulates faster during pregnancy — a proper rest day mid-trip means she'll have energy for the Paros day trip and Santorini. Resist filling it.
Getting there
Early SeaJets ferry Naxos → Paros: departs ~8:30–9am, 45 min. Tickets ~€15–20/person. Buy the night before at the port or online. SeaJets is fine for this short hop — 45 min is easy.
Morning — Kolymbithres beach
Water taxi from Parikia port to Kolymbithres (~€5/person, 10 min). Extraordinary smooth granite rock formations rising from turquoise sea — completely unique in the Cyclades.
Swim between the rocks, explore small coves. Arrive early to beat boat tours. The rocks create natural shade pockets.
Afternoon — Naoussa harbor
Water taxi or bus back to Parikia, then bus to Naoussa (~20 min, €2). Arrive 1:30–2pm.
Lunch on Naoussa harbor — Barbarossa or Taverna Glafkos for fresh seafood. One of the best meals of the trip. Budget €45–55 for two.
Walk Naoussa's alleys after lunch — Venetian port ruins, whitewashed lanes, boutique galleries. 🤰 Cobblestones throughout — flat supportive shoes essential.
Return
Return ferry Paros → Naxos: aim for 5:30–6:30pm departure. Back in Naxos by 7pm. Light dinner or easy evening in.
💡 Day trip verdict
This covers the best of Paros in one day — Kolymbithres in the morning (unique rock formations, nowhere else like it in Greece) and Naoussa harbor at lunch (best dining on the island). What you miss vs staying overnight is Naoussa at night — a real sacrifice, but saving 3 hotel nights is very significant.
Morning
Last Naxos morning — coffee and a pastry from the bakery near the port. Walk the waterfront one final time. Check out and store bags at your hotel.

🌋 Santorini
The grand finale · Caldera views · Oia 📍 sunset · Sep 2–5
Daily budget
€200–260 / couple
3 nights
🤰 Pregnancy notes for Santorini: Stay in Fira 📍 (not Oia 📍) — 40–60% cheaper and flatter to walk. Use taxis between Fira 📍 and Oia 📍 (€20–25), not the crowded buses. Skip the Skaros Rock descent, the 300 steps to Ammoudi port, and the Ancient Thira hike — all involve steep uneven terrain. The caldera boat tour on a small kaiki boat (not a catamaran) is the most pregnancy-friendly activity on the island.
Where you're staying — Elios Cave Suites 📍 ✅
Check into Elios Cave Suites 📍 in Foinikiá village near Oia 📍 — confirmed ✅, Sep 2–5, $360 total. Taxi from Athinios Port to Elios ~€25–30. Check-in from 3pm.
Breakfast is not included — plan for a local café or bakery each morning (~€8–12 for two). Foinikiá has a small village bakery nearby.
Foinikiá is a quiet authentic village ~15 min walk to Oia 📍 center and ~10 min drive to the caldera and Fira 📍. Taxis are your main transport — budget €20–30 per ride. Email Elios ahead of arrival to mention the pregnancy and confirm check-in details.
Afternoon — caldera walk
Taxi to Fira 📍 (~€25–30 from Elios) then walk the caldera path north toward Firostefani and Imerovigli — mostly flat, paved, extraordinary views at every turn.
🤰 Skip the Skaros Rock descent — steep loose gravel, no handrail. The clifftop views above Imerovigli are just as spectacular and entirely safe.
Evening
Walk back to Oia 📍 (30 min along caldera path) or taxi back to Foinikiá (~€12–15). Dinner near Elios or in Oia 📍 — Roka in Oia 📍 for good grilled food at fair prices. Tonight is a gentle settling-in evening, save the splurge for Sep 4.
💡 Tip
Get Elios hotel to give you their recommended local taxi contact on arrival — this is essential for your Santorini days. Pre-book taxis for the Oia 📍 sunset day (Sep 3) as they fill up fast at peak times.
Morning — Perissa Beach 📍
Perissa Beach 📍: dramatic black volcanic sand, sheer Mesa Vouno cliffs, warm clear water. Taxi from Elios ~€25–30 (Perissa is on the south side of the island — easier from Foinikiá than from Fira 📍). Or bus from Fira 📍 bus station if you'd rather go via town.
Sunbeds recommended (~€15–20/pair) — black sand absorbs more heat than white sand, shade umbrella is essential here.
🤰 Skip the Ancient Thira hike (steep 30-min climb in direct sun). Morning beach, mandatory midday hotel rest, then Oia 📍 in the afternoon.
Afternoon & sunset — Oia 📍
Taxi to Oia 📍 by 4pm (~€20–25). Walk the alleys at a relaxed pace — blue-domed churches, cave-house boutiques. Oia 📍's streets are mostly flat and easy to navigate.
Kasteli castle ruins for sunset: position by 5:30pm. Find a seat on the low walls. The 7:30–8pm September sunset genuinely lives up to every photograph you've ever seen. One of the great romantic moments in the world.
🤰 Skip the 300 steps down to Ammoudi fishing port — steep, uneven, no handrail. Dinner in Oia 📍 at Roka restaurant (great grilled meats, flat access) or taxi back to Fira 📍.
💡 Pregnancy tip
🤰 Taxi between Fira 📍 and Oia 📍 (€20–25) rather than the bus — buses are standing-room only, hot, and crowded in peak season. Worth every cent on a full day like this.
Morning — caldera boat tour
🤰 Book a small traditional kaiki boat tour (not a catamaran) — calmer caldera water, smaller group, same views. ~€35–50/person. Book in person at Fira 📍's old port the evening before to save €20–30 vs online.
Tour covers: hot springs at Palea Kameni (warm, shallow — confirm with OB), cruise past Nea Kameni volcano, and caldera views of Oia 📍 and Fira 📍 from the water.
The view of the caldera walls from the sea — with Oia 📍 and Fira 📍 perched above you — is the perspective that makes Santorini click. You're literally sailing inside a collapsed volcano. Back by 12:30pm.
Afternoon — Pyrgos village
Taxi to Pyrgos (~15 min, €15) — Santorini's highest, most traditional village. Byzantine castle ruins, marble paths, virtually no tour buses. This is the Santorini most visitors never see.
Franco's Bar inside an old windmill — panoramic views of the entire island. The most atmospheric pit-stop on Santorini.
Back to hotel by 4:30pm. Tonight is your splurge dinner — caldera-view table, local Assyrtiko white wine, fresh seafood. Budget €60–80 for two.
💡 Pregnancy tip
🤰 The kaiki boat stays in calm caldera water the entire time — the most pregnancy-friendly activity on the island. This is the most activity-packed Santorini day so keep the evening simple and seated.
Last Santorini morning
Coffee on the caldera terrace. Final walk along the rim path. Pack and check out — store bags at hotel while you have lunch.
Lunch with a caldera view — your last meal in Santorini. Pick the spot you've been eyeing all trip.
Flying to Athens
Taxi to Santorini Airport (JTR) — tiny airport, arrive 90 min before departure. Afternoon Aegean or Olympic Air flight to Athens (~€50–80/person, ~45 min).
🤰 Flying confirmed — far more comfortable than the 5–8hr Santorini→Piraeus 📍 ferry. Book as early as possible — September routes fill up fast.
Land Athens. Go directly to your airport hotel — do not go into the city center tonight.
Athens airport hotel — Peri's Hotel 📍
Peri's Hotel 📍 (confirmed, $30/night) — free shuttle pickup from the airport, 10 min drive to hotel. Contact them before your Sep 5 arrival with your flight details to arrange pickup.
Walk 15 min to Artemida town for a final Greek dinner — plenty of tavernas and souvlaki spots. Early night by 9pm.
🤰 6am departure Sep 6 = need to leave hotel ~3:30am. Confirm shuttle time with hotel when you contact them. Breakfast included but not at 3am — grab snacks the night before.
💡 Critical action
📞 Contact Peri's Hotel 📍 before travel with your Sep 5 arrival flight details — they need advance notice to arrange the free airport pickup. Also confirm a 3:30am shuttle to the airport for your 6am Sep 6 departure. The hotel can be hard to find without their pickup so don't skip this step.
